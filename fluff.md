## The Cosmere

### Adonalsium & The Shattering

A long time ago, in Cosmere terms, 16 people came together to shatter a being of various intents and energy and took those into themselves. That being was Adonalsium and that is the impetus of the Cosmere's struggle at large. Each of these concepts, a Shard, was held by a different person, a Vessel, where they all held in agreement to not interact.

Of course, what good is an agreement if it's not broken?

### The Shards 

The main three worlds explored here are

* Roshar, the home of the Stormlight Archives
* Scadrial, the home of Mistborn
* Nalthis, the home of Warbreaker

Each of these has at least one Shard associated with them and the intent. 

Nalthis is the home of Endowment, the intent of which is a little vague in lore, but appears to be about giving abilities or talents. The Vessel for Endowment is a being known as Edgli - a mysterious woman who seems both comfortaing and easily frustrated by some other actors in the Cosmere

Scadrial has two Shards, Ruin, whose Vessel by Ati and Preservation whose Vessel is Leras. Anything more than that and I'd have to put more spoilers on this presentation. Maybe once a few people finish some books around me, I'll feel comfortable doing that.

Roshar has two Shards - Honor and Cultivation, whose Vessels are unknown by Words of Radiance, as well as another shard in another nearby planet called Odium. These three drive a lot of the plot of Stormlight Archives, so I'll RAFO that too.


### Investitiure - The Cosmere's Magic

Each world has its own "magic system" influenced by the Shard's Intent of the planet they're on. This magic system may appear as several different ways. By using pieces of the Shard's energy, they're able to do fantastic abilites that follow distinct rules that are consistent around each planet.

Each planet experiences the energy required to these abilities differently, however, this energy is collectively known as Investure. On one planet, magical Breaths are the form of energy needed to use abilities while on another the passing of an in-land hurricane recharges this Investure as Stormlight.

### Worldhopping

I mean, if you have all these worlds and these magic systems, what happens if you combine them? There are ways, very tricky and not at all easy, for people to move between these worlds. The people capable of doing this are known as Worldhoppers and can move themselves, sometimes their magic, and sometimes their overall essence between the worlds of the Cosmere, often with varying effects. The most famous Worldhopper is an interesting individual known, usually, as Hoid.

## Magic Systems

### Surgebinding - Roshar

Roshar has the Nahel Bond - the ablity to make an oath and keep it to gain more abilities from the ideals of Honor itself. These bonds with pieces of the world known as Spren allow access to fragments of power. A Windrunner, for instance, can change the affect of gravity around them and others based on how many oaths, or Ideals, they have spoken and kept. These affects are called Surges and they are effectively known as Surgebinding - the ability to call the Surges to themselves.

### Allomancy - Scadrial

Scadrial has several forms of magic, but Allomancy is the biggest one in the first few books. This magical system relies on consuming metals to be able to do fantastic abilities with pushing or pulling energy once specific metals are consumed and "burned". For instance, a Rioter that consumes Zinc who then uses it can inflame emotions such as increasing desire, anger, or determination. There are several metals that all have different abilites - Pewter increasing physical abilities, Iron pulling other metals towards them, and many more. Kelsier, in Mistborn: The Final Empire, explains it more in depth but suffice it to say that this "magic" system follows the same logic of pushing and pull metals to create fantastic abilites. How this relates to one of the Shards is for the reader to discover.

### Awakening - Nalthis

On Nalthis, giving and taking of magical essence in the form of Breaths is from Endowment. They can give and receive these Breaths from other Nalthians but they can also use them to "Awaken" inanimate objects but endowing them with a certain number of breaths and a phrase. During the prologue of Warbreaker, a small straw doll is endowed with several Breaths and given the command to "Fetch Keys".
