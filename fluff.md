## The Cosmere

### Adonalsium & The Shattering

A long time ago, in Cosmere terms, 16 people came together to shatter a being of various intents and energy and took those into themselves. That being was Adonalsium and that is the impetus of the Cosmere's struggle at large. Each of these concepts, a Shard, was held by a different person, a Vessel, where they all held in agreement to not interact.

Of course, what good is an agreement if it's not broken?

### The Shards 

The main three worlds explored here are

* Roshar, the home of the Stormlight Archives
* Scadrial, the home of Mistborn
* Nalthis, the home of Warbreaker

Each of these has at least one Shard associated with them and the intent. 

Nalthis is the home of Endowment, the intent of which is a little vague in lore, but appears to be about giving abilities or talents. The Vessel for Endowment is a being known as Edgli - a mysterious woman who seems both comfortaing and easily frustrated by some other actors in the Cosmere

Scadrial has two Shards, Ruin, whose Vessel by Ati and Preservation whose Vessel is Leras. Anything more than that and I'd have to put more spoilers on this presentation. Maybe once a few people finish some books around me, I'll feel comfortable doing that.

Roshar has two Shards - Honor and Cultivation, whose Vessels are unknown by Words of Radiance, as well as another shard in another nearby planet called Odium. These three drive a lot of the plot of Stormlight Archives, so I'll RAFO that too.


### Investitiure - The Cosmere's Magic

Each world has its own "magic system" influenced by the Shard's Intent of the planet they're on. This magic system may appear as several different ways. By using pieces of the Shard's energy, they're able to do fantastic abilites that follow distinct rules that are consistent around each planet.

Each planet experiences the energy required to these abilities differently, however, this energy is collectively known as Investure. On one planet, magical Breaths are the form of energy needed to use abilities while on another the passing of an in-land hurricane recharges this Investure as Stormlight.

### Worldhopping

I mean, if you have all these worlds and these magic systems, what happens if you combine them? There are ways, very tricky and not at all easy, for people to move between these worlds. The people capable of doing this are known as Worldhoppers and can move themselves, sometimes their magic, and sometimes their overall essence between the worlds of the Cosmere, often with varying effects. The most famous Worldhopper is an interesting individual known, usually, as Hoid.

### The Landscape

Roshar has a varied landscape, from the Eastern rugged Unclaimed Hills to the farmer-friendly lands between and into the deserts, islands, and lakes between, ending in the strange, grass covered lands of Shinovar in the West.

Notably, in the southeartned part of the map, south of the Unclaimed Hills is the Shattered Plains, a very important location for the first few books and several weeks travel from the capital of Alethkar - Kholinar. 

### The Highstorms

Why list the lands from east to west when talking about them? There's a good reason; the Highstorm.

The Highstorm is a land-bound hurricane that regularly moves every few days from East to West that affects the flora and fauna of the land, recharges Stormlight in spheres, and even affects architecture, by people building walls stormward (or East facing) to absorb the storms will leaving more finesse architecture leeward (or West facing).

This frightening storm is incredibly powerful, able to throw bolders, drown small riverbeds and pools, and blow away items not secured. People left out in the Highstorms are often injured if not killed by the storm itself.

The Highstorms themselves start stronger in the East, near the Shattered Plains and Alethkar before becoming weakened and merely a rainstorm by the time it gets to Shinovar.

### The Nations

There are 31 Nations during the Era of Solitude, some of them incredibly small and some of them multiple sizes larger than other nations. The most referenced nations during the Era of Solitude are:

* Thaylenah - This naval mercantile nation is home to Queen Fen and the merchant councils and the Thaylen Navy. Strongly focused on trade and strength at sea, they are allies to many of the main nations - so that they can trade with them. They also have guilds who are focused on creating Fabrials not otherwise seen in the world.

* Alethkar - The biggest and most powerful nation of the planet of Roshar. The Alethi people are known as disciplined but warlike and have spread their major religion, Vorinism, to most of the rest of the world. The capital city, Kholinar, is one of the oldest cities as it existed thousands of years ago during the time of the Ten Kingdoms

* Kharbranth - Technically, a city-state near the Frostlands, Kharbranth's ruler - King Taravangian - is well loved by his people. The City of Bells, as it's known, has the largest library on Roshar, the Palanaeum, and is well regarded for its hospitals and medics. As small as it is, it rarely engages in war but provides medical attention to all sides of a conflict. 

* Shinovar - A strange nation far to the west whose people look different than most of the rest of Roshar, having large eyes and paler skin. Not too much is known about Shinovar except that they have both chickens and strange ground covering. 

### The Current Era

The current era, known as the Era of Solitude for this discussion, takes place around year 1174, about 7 years after the Vengeance Pact is signed.

At this point, the Alethi and the Parshendi are at war with each other in the Shattered Plains, with Dalinar Kholin pushing his troops closer and closer to the middle of the Shattered Plains.

The Recreance, the moment when the original Knights Radiant gave up their powers, is nearly 2000 years in the past, the Radiants gone from Roshar forever. Or so it seems.

## Terms, Phrases, and Cultural Notes

### Eye Color

Especally in Alethkar, but across the entire world, eye color determines your rank. Lighteyes, those with blue or green eyes, are able to hold higher positions and rulership over those with brown eyes, known as darkeyes. This division has caused a rift between the two, with darkeyes often hating lighteyes leadership and lighteyes looking down on the darkeyes as lesser.

### Spren

Everything has a spirit to it called Spren. The wind has spren, fire has spren, joy, glory, anger, and all kinds of emotions have spren. They manifest differently depending on the type of spren, with flamespren looking like little dancing people and gloryspren look like little balls of glowing gold light. There are intelligent and powerful spren, especially those working with Knights Radiant.

### Shardplate and Shardblades

Shards are powerful relics of the past. Shardblades can cut through anything - stone, people, and other items. However, it cannot cut through a Shardplate. Shardplate is normally very heavy (1400 lbs in this game!) except those that are wearing it while it's imbued with Stormlight find it easy to move around in and use.

When a shardblade cuts through a person it doesn't leave a physical wound, but when a strike is lethal, such as through a spine, the person's eyes turn to smoke as they collapse without any physical wound. A non-lethal hit, such as swinging through an arm, causes it to turn gray and lifelessly hang. Since they have no resistance, they are very powerful in war, their scarcity - each nation only have a few, or maybe even 1 - is what prevents them from being huge weapons of war.

### Vorinism

This is the major religion that almost every nation in the East at least gives token reverence to, with it being significant in places such as Alethkar and Jah Keved. Vorinism teaches that the Almighty and his Ten Heralds are in an eternal struggle with the Voidbringers.

Each person has a Calling they strive to meet in life. Virtuous humans who meet their Calling go to the Tranquiline Halls after death to fight the Voidbringers and send them to Damnation to reclaim it. The humans who lead a virtuous life but don't meet their life's Calling are left in dreamless sleep until the War for Heaven is won.

The voidbringers and those who lead sinful lives go to Damnation, a place of eternal burning full of torture, evil spren, and monsters.


## Notable Characters

### The Protagonists

* King Elhokar - King of Alethkar, nephew to Dalinar Kholin, son of Navani Kholin. Created the Vengeance Pact to unite the Highprinces and go to war against the Parshendi - the people that had killed his father.

* Dalinar Kholin - General of the Kholin armies. A notable warrior, including fighting off Chasmfiends and leading the charge from the front with his troops. A dangerous man sometimes called "the Blackthorn".

* Navani Kholin - Mother of the current King and a noted scholar. Her patronage and suggestions continue to improve Fabrial - fantastic devices powered by Stormlight.

* Jasnah Kholin - A scholar and a well-known heretic to the major religion of the land, Vorinism. Elegence with a pen and shuts out most other people except her family that she is devoted to.

* Kaladin - Opening protagaonist and member of the forced-labor Bridge Four for crimes he was framed for by Meridas Amaram. Quick with a spear and a natural leader, he eventually finds ways to deal with his overwhelming sense of despair.

* Bridge Four - A collective of forced labor - mostly slave punishment, who are to run bridges between the cracked Shattered Plains to allow the armies to pass. Bridge duty is notably high in mortality rate due to opposing armies targeting them. Bridge Four is a focus of the stories as it's the bridge crew that Kaladin is part of.

### The Antagonists

* Parshendi - A warlike people with thick, orange carapace that fight in pairs. They notably oppose the Alethi highprinces at the Shattered Plains and have strength and speed that seems to surpass their physical frame. Notably, the Parshendi sent the Assassin in White to kill the former king, which triggered the Vengeance Pact by the current king.

* Meridas Amaram - A distinguished general in the Alethi armies known by many darkeyes to be a hero. Wield shard and plate that was earned by Kaladin, after having framed Kaladin and disposing of his unit. Sent Kaladin to the Bridge Crews.

* Szeth-son-son-Vallano - The assassin in white, killed the former king at the request of the Parshendi. He is from the tiny, neutral nation of Shinovar. He is a Truthless, a title given to him by the shamantic leaders of the Shin. He considers walking on stones to be profane and has other religious beliefs that does not line up with the common Vorinism religion.


## Magic Systems

### Surgebinding - Roshar

Roshar has the Nahel Bond - the ablity to make an oath and keep it to gain more abilities from the ideals of Honor itself. These bonds with pieces of the world known as Spren allow access to fragments of power. A Windrunner, for instance, can change the affect of gravity around them and others based on how many oaths, or Ideals, they have spoken and kept. These affects are called Surges and they are effectively known as Surgebinding - the ability to call the Surges to themselves.

### Allomancy - Scadrial

Scadrial has several forms of magic, but Allomancy is the biggest one in the first few books. This magical system relies on consuming metals to be able to do fantastic abilities with pushing or pulling energy once specific metals are consumed and "burned". For instance, a Rioter that consumes Zinc who then uses it can inflame emotions such as increasing desire, anger, or determination. There are several metals that all have different abilites - Pewter increasing physical abilities, Iron pulling other metals towards them, and many more. Kelsier, in Mistborn: The Final Empire, explains it more in depth but suffice it to say that this "magic" system follows the same logic of pushing and pull metals to create fantastic abilites. How this relates to one of the Shards is for the reader to discover.

### Awakening - Nalthis

On Nalthis, giving and taking of magical essence in the form of Breaths is from Endowment. They can give and receive these Breaths from other Nalthians but they can also use them to "Awaken" inanimate objects but endowing them with a certain number of breaths and a phrase. During the prologue of Warbreaker, a small straw doll is endowed with several Breaths and given the command to "Fetch Keys".
