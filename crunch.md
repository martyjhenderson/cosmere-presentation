# Crunch

## Core Dice System

The main part of the dice system is a traditional polyhedral set — a d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, and d20. In addition, there is a "plot die" that can cause opportunities, a boon, or complications, a bane.

### Assembling the Dice Pool

The dice pool is a skill die — derived from a stat — plus a d20, rolled against a target number set by the GM. The GM determines success or failure based on that target number. If the "Stakes are Raised," an opportunity or a failure can add a complexity to the roll from the plot die.

### Opportunities & Complications

When the plot die results in a complication, it can add a small amount to the total roll — either +2 or +4, depending on the roll. This may cause a success, even though you roll a complication.

Opportunities and Complications generate effects determined by the player and the GM. For instance, an opportunity on a battlefield may result in an allied force arriving to help turn the tide, while a complication may introduce enemy combatants. Even if you succeed with a complication, things will happen! You may successfully pry open a door in a highstorm, but you might lose your favorite prying dagger to the winds in the process!

### Roll It All

Whenever you make a check that involves multiple dice, such as an attack roll, you roll all the dice together - the d20, the damage die, and the plot die if you are Raising the Stakes. No need for checking to hit before rolling damage!


## Character Creation

### Choose your world

The Cosmere is vast, many named planets such as Roshar, where Stormlight Archives takes places, Scadrial - the home of Allomancy, to even the strange planet of Komashi with its hion technology. As of right now, early 2026, only Roshar is available (with Scadrial and Mistborn coming Fall 2026). So selecting your world is easy; you're from Roshar. 

### Ancestry

One of the first things you will choose is your ancestry. of which Roshar offers two - Humans or Singers.

### Culture

Culture is pulled separately from your Ancestry, simply because you can be any ancestry and be affected by your culture. You get up to two cultural experiences which are mostly regional in Stormlight. Are you a human who grew up in bureaucracy of Azir? A singer who helped Theylan merchants? Your culture affects your knowledge and language and probably your character's outlook on the world.

### Starting Path

An interesting feature of the Cosmere RPG is that classes aren't linear but more of a series of "flowcharts" you can hop between, but you start down one of the paths. Initially, you have to start down a Heroic Path with Radiant Paths becoming unlocked later - no one starts Radiant!

In Stormlight, the Heroic paths are

Agent - This is your sneaky, manipulative type. Their special ability is the ability to reroll the Plot Die once per round.

Envoy - This is your face character. They can make one character Determined, a condition explained later in this presentation.

Hunter - Ranged attacker and outdoorsy type. They can choose someone to focus one and gain Advantage against.

Leaders - Inspiring commander to others, almost bard-like in traditional TTRPG flavoring. Their special ability is the ability to give a d4 to an ally's check.

Scholar - Thinkers and intelligence focused folks - both crafters and medics come from this Path. They get the ability to flex some skill points around.

Warrior - Your classic in-your-face type problem solver. They get specialty stances that alter how you do combat.

Each of these also have specialties as your progress down the path

### Attributes

There are six attributes that you have that are further derived for other skills and states, but those attributes are Strength, Speed, Intellect, Willpower, Awareness, and Presence. They go from 0 to 5 total, but at this step in character building, nothing can be higher than 3. Initially, you start with 12 points and the recommended distribution is 0, 1, 2, 3, 3, 3 but you are welcome to do however you want - go for the min-maxed 0, 0, 3, 3, 3, 3 if you are really feeling it. You cannot have negative numbers for attributes

The attributes align pretty much along the expected definitions:

* Strength impacts using heavy weapons, carrying things, health, and similar tasks

* Speed affects how fast and far you move plus how you use more agile weapons

* Intellect affects your skills and thoughtful checks

* Willpower affects how well you can recover and focus among other things.

* Awareness is how you see the world, which also impacts how well you sense things (but not limited to vision alone!)

* Presence is your force of personality which has more impact on derived abilities to be covered later.

### Skills and Talents

There are 18 total skills to choose from when building a character with a couple more added on for some Paths as you level up. For character creation, you get one skill rank based on your Path and then 4 more ranks you can apply across any of the 18. At character creation, you cannot have a skill higher than 2.

Each skill has an associated attribute. For instance, Deception uses Presence while Light Weaponry uses Speed.

When rolling the skill, it'll be your ranks in that skill and the score for the attribute (plus any conditional modifiers). This is what generates the number to put in the box. 

### Investiture

At character creation, no one is Invested. No one is stopping you from opening the pages to the Radiant Paths and admiring the cool things they can do or the killer art on that page, but it won't impact your starting character - all Heroic Paths can lead to Radiant Paths.

### Expertise

Expertise is one of the final things. This is a very narrow subject that you know a lot about. It should be a word or a very, very short phrase such as "Engineering" or "Dances". There's a ton of suggestions in the book, but it's safe to say the choices are pretty open and endless. Your expertises can bypass checks - if you had expertise in Dances, for instance, you might be able to fit in at a ball scene where someone else may need to roll Deception to fit in.

You start with 2 expertises plus your Intellect score. 

### Talents

As you choose your Heroic Path, you will get an initial talent, such as Warrior's Vigilant Stance. As you level up later, you will get to take more talents down the talent tree, but everyone gets an initial talent from their Heroic Path.

Also, based on your Ancestry, you get an Ancestry Talent. There are quite a few options, but the one to really make note of is Human's "Ancestry Bonus Talent" which breaks a few rules - you can choose 1 talent either in progressing your current Heroic Path or the Intial Talent of another Heroic Path. Singers get a special talent tree unique to Singers they can choose from during character creation.

### Equipment

Based on your background, you get a starting kit - which may also have intangible benefits! Your kit will also have "marks" which are Roshar's currency that you can also immediately spend on other such gear.

At this point, it'd be good to note that if your armor has a "deflect" value to throw it on your sheet. It'll be used later to figure out how much armor you can ignore, but it's easier to mark it down when you get your equipment

### Story

Here's where we answer "how do I level?" and similar questions. 

Two important things to start with are purpose and obstacle. These two things are what drives your character and what stops them from becoming everything they want to be. Obstacle can be a self-inflicted issue like pride or it can be an external factor like not being able to do certain jobs due to the color of your eyes.

Goals. Goals are what make and break this game and why it'll struggle to be episodic like Organized Play. A goal is a subjective thing you're going for in game. They are things like "escape the Bridge Crews" or the Radiant-specific "swear the First Ideal" or even "Acquire a Shardblade". You have three boxes next to your goal that your GM will tell you to check off as you get closer to completing it. Once you complete a goal, you get a reward, which may be unlocking Radiant Paths, getting a specific possession, titles and grants, or even new relationships with powerful people.

You can also start to fill out other finer details like Occupation, Relationships (either in the party or outside the party), Loyalties, Personality, Appearance, and even what Radiant Order you might want to consider.

## The Math

### Resource Pools

You get three resource pools, though only two initially (the third one comes into play if you become Invested as a Radiant)

* Health, how much damage you can take, is 10 plus your Strength (plus any other outside bonuses). If your Strength is 0, then you have 10 hit points.

* Focus is 2 + Willpower (plus any other outside bonuses). If your Willpower were 2, you'd have 4 total Focus.

Investiture is something you do not get until you become Radiant and take a Radiant path. However, if you wanted to calculate it early: 

* Investiture is 2 + higher of Awareness or Presence (plus any outside bonuses). An Awareness of 2 and a Presence of 1 means you'd have 4 Investiture (an Awareness of 2 is higher than a Presence of 1 and then you add your base 2).

### Defenses

You get three defenses - one is for physical injury, one is for mental activity, and one is for your spiritual being. 

* Physical defense is 10 plus Strength plus Speed (plus any outside bonuses).

* Cognitive Defense is 10 plus Intellect plus Willpower (plus any outside bonuses).

* Spiritual is 10 plus Awareness plus Presence (plus any outside bonuses).

## Recovery Die

Based on your Willpower attribute

* Willpower 0 - 1d4
* Willpower 1-2 - 1d6
* Willpower 3-4 - 1d8
* Willpower 5-6 - 1d10
* Willpower 7-8 - 1d12
* Willpower 9+ - 1d20

## Speed

Based on your Speed attribute

* Speed 0 - 20' per action
* Speed 1-2 - 25' per action
* Speed 3-4 - 30' per action
* Speed 5-6 - 40' per action
* Speed 7-8 - 60' per action
* Speed 9+ - 80' per action

## Senses

Senses range is only relevant when your main sense is obscured. For instance, if it's in a dark room, your senses range kicks in to tell how far you can sense things are at.

Based on your Awareness attributes

* Awareness 0 - 5'
* Awareness 1-2 - 10'
* Awareness 3-4 - 20'
* Awareness 5-6 - 50'
* Awareness 7-8 - 100'
* Awareness 9+ - Unaffected

## Combat

### Initiative and Turn Order

Turns are very simple and, I believe, totally stolen from Rollmaster.

On your turn, you decide if you're going to have a Fast Turn or a Slow Turn. All PCs go before enemies in each turn order. You can choose a different speed each round and you don't even need to decide at the beginning of the round. You can wait until another PC goes fast that you, too, want to go fast!

If you go with a fast turn, you only get two actions on your turn. If you go slow, you get three. Regardless of speed, you get a single reaction in a given turn.

This leaves the turn order as

1. Fast PCs
2. Fast enemies
3. Slow PCs
4. Slow Enemies

It's pretty clean cut, but there are cards available to indicate to the table what you're doing.

### Actions on Your Turn

There are a lot of actions you can take on your turn! Everyone has the same basic actions before their Paths grant any additional ones. Each action can only be used once a round, unless noted otherwise.

Things that use your reaction:

* Aid - Spend 1 Focus and give advantage on a (reasonable) check an ally is making
* Avoid Danger - Make an Agility Test to avoid eminant danger, like a boulder falling on you
* Dodge - Before an enemy targets you with an attack, spend 1 Focus to grant them Disadvantge on the attack
* Reactive Strike - when an enemy chooses to leave your reach, spend 1 Focus to do a melee attack against their physical defense. 

Things that are free actions:

* Banter - Talking is free. Arguably, this could include crying. This cannot be used to change attitudes or focus - those are Use a Skill actions.
* Drop - Let go of anything you're holding

Things that take a single action:

* Brace - Hide behind reasonable cover to give disadvantage on attacks. This can be used with a shield pretty nicely!
* Disengage - Move 5 feet without causing Reactive Strikes
* Gain Advantage - Use a skill to give advantage on your next, _different_ skill. For instance, you might use Deception to feint giving your Heavy Weaponry skill advantage.
* Interact - This is any time you need to interact with an object, like closing a door, retrieve an item from your bag, give somethign to an ally, or as the rules say "quickly scarf down pancakes"
* Move - Move up to your Move speed (Note: this action can be used more than once a round)
* Ready (plus the cost of the action you're readying) - Create a trigger and an action that happens because of the trigger. If the trigger is met, you can immediately take the associated action. Plesae note that this does not allow you to take the same action twice in one round (e.g. you can't do a Strike then ready an action for a second Strike)
* Strike - Perform a attack with a weapon you have in hand against an enemy's physical defense. You can use this multiple times in one round with the caveat that each strike must be made with a different hand AND each strike after the first costs 2 Focus.
* Use a Skill - Use one of your skills to perform an action, like Medicine to check wounds or Stealth to sneak around.

Things that take two actions:

* Grapple - Perform an Athletics test against Physical Defense of an enemy. If you succeed, they are Restrained until they are no longer in your reach, your free-action drop the grapple, or you go unconscious.
* Recover - Roll a recovery die to get back health and focus. (Note: this can only be used once per scene!)
* Shove - Perform an Athletics test against the Physical Defense of an enemy. If you succeed, push them 5 ft away horizontally. This movement can end a grapple!

### Making an Attack

There are three main steps in making an attack.

1. Choose your target
2. Throw all the dice - attack and damage
3. Resolve damage

Choosing your target ensures that they're a valid target - are they in melee if you're using a sword, can you see your target if you're shooting an arrow, can you sense the target in your environment. This is to ensure your target is valid.

You'll then make a skill test against the correct defense. A heavy sword would be Heavy Weaponry against Physical Defense, but some special attacks might be a different Defence or use a different skill.

Resolving damage has 4 potential outcomes:

* Miss - your test fails and nothing happens
* Graze - you'd miss but you can spend 1 Focus (per target) to do damage equal to the amount rolled on the die (no other bonuses added)
* Hit - You do damage equal to the die plus the modifier for the skill you used
* Critical Hit - You can spend an Opportunity to change the hit into a critical. This means you ignore what you rolled on the die and do max damage from the roll.

Note: A 20 is always an Opportunity and a 1 is always a Complication!


### Armor Deflection

Defences are calculated earlier, but your armor can reduce the amount of damage you take by their deflection value. For instance, Chain armor has a deflect value of 2, so it reduces all incoming damage by 2.

### Conditions and Status

A variety of things can cause Conditions. For instance, speaking the First Ideal makes you Empowered for the scene where being grappled makes you Restrained. You can only be under each contition once (you can't be double Prone for instance!)

Condition:

* Afflicted - you take damage at the end of the turn base don the afflicted status. If you're Afflcited [1 vital] you take 1 damage at the of the turn. You can be afflicted by this one more than once. 
* Determined - Once time, when you fail a test, you can add an Opportunity to the result.
* Disoriented - No reaction, you're obscured, and all perception is at disadvantage.
* Empowered - Gain advantage on all tests and Investure refills at the start of each turn.
* Enhanced - You can the bonus to the attribute listed, such as Enhanced [+3 Willpower] increases your Willpower by 3. However, it never affacets defenses, max health, max focus, or max investure. You can have multiple attribtues Enhanced at the same time.
* Exhuasted - You reduce all skill tests by the amount in brackets. This is cumulative! However, exhaustion reduces by 1 for each Long Rest you take. For instance, Exhuasted [-2] would apply -2 to all skill tests, but would go down to 0 after two long rests.
* Focused - Reduce focus costs by 1
* Immobilized - Your movement becomes 0 and you can't move or be moved
* Prone - You are Slowed and melee attacks have advantage against you. You can Brace without cover. Standing up is a free action, but your movement rate is reduced by 5 until your next turn.
* Restrained - Your movement becomes 0 and you have disadvantage on all checks that do not involve you escaping your bonds. GM fiat applies to what checks apply.
* Slowed - Your movement is halved, rounded up.
* Stunned - You lose any reactions and on your turn you get two fewer actions and don't gain a reaction.
* Surprised - You lose any reactions, don't get reactions at the start of combat/on your turn, can't take a Fast Turn, and gain one fewer action.
* Unconscious - Your movement rate becomes 0, you can't move or communicate, and you're unaware of your surroundings.


## Leveling Up

### How to Level Up

A GM decides, based on story milestones, when you can level up your character. This triggers the mechanics based on the Character Advancement chart. There are no XP or "level every X hours/sessions" - it's entirely up to the GM when your skills and experiences are ready to become more, in a numerical sense.

In addition as you complete personal goals, you will gain rewards that can also provide new aspects and powers to your character.

### Character Advancement

There is a Character Advancement table that shows all the benefits you get, but a simple look from 1-2 that you gain no new attribute points, +5 health, a max skill rank of 2, and 1 new talent.

The new talent can be on your existing Heroic Path or on a new Heroic Path (where you start with the Initial Trait) or, if you've met your goal for attracting a spren or speaking Ideals, a Radiant Path. This will unlock new abilities and possibly other status changes - each talent is unique!

You also must pick talents lineary and cannot skip around the chart - it's more of a flowchart than a menu. Some talents also have prequisites that must be met.

### Tiers of Play

There are some intentional caps every 5 levels for things such as max skill ranks, the number of skills and amount of health you gain, and suggestions to the GM on how difficult to make a given scene. 

In addition, there are premade adventures and scenarios from Brotherwise Games and others that use a recommended tier.

These tiers go 1-5, banding for each 5 levels of play. 

Tier:
1 - Levels 1-5
2 - Levels 6-10
3 - Levels 11-15
4 - Levels 16-20
5 - Level 21+

Although you can exceed level 20, the main game system is designed around a level 20 max and things get a bit wobbly past that.

### Rewards

One way they allowed both Radiant and non-Radiant character to have an impact is through Rewards. Radiants will have to focus more on strengthening their bond to their spren and their Ideals. Heroic Paths instead build on getting powerful items, connections, and building an external legend. 

For instance, while a Radiant will work on a Second Ideal, a Heroic character will gain a Shadeblade or a title and land or a connection to a powerful member of society. 


## Equipment and Items

### Weapons

Weapons come with a special chart to indicate which skill to use, damage, range, and special traits, and expert traits.

Expert traits only apply if you have expertise in that weapon, such as Knives having the offhand and throw traits when you're an expert.

A knife does 1d4 damage, melee, that uses Light Weaponry, and has the discrete trait where a Shortbow has the d6 damage, ranged [80/320], two-handed, and the expert trait of Two-Handed that also uses Light Weaponry. 

Ranged specifically announces the basic range, where you roll like normal, or max range, where you roll at disadvantage. For example, [80/320] has up to 80' at normal range but 81'-320' at disadvantage.

### Armor

Armor has a chart much like weapons, except it has a deflect value, or how much damage it mitigates. The most common trait is Cumbersome where if you do not meet the strength in the brackets, you are slowed while wearing it. For instance, Full Plate has Cumbersome [5] meaning you are slowed unless you have a strength of 5, which is not easy to get to!

### Shardblades and Shardplate

Shardblades and Shardplate are special, unique pieces of weapon and armor that can only be earned through rewards.  

Shardplate has a special set of traits but the most important being "Charges". While it has charges, normally 4, it gives enhanced speed and strength, protection from Invested abilities, and it can deflect shardblades. It also, when it runs out of charges, can be recharged, though it takes time. They're also breakable - an Opportunity against the Shardplate can be used to reduce it's charges by 1, getting it closer to losing it's enhanced attritbutes and protection traits. 

Radiant Shardplate is a bit different, but digging too deep into that spoils books past Words of Radiance. RAFO.

Shardblades are one of the few items that do spirit damage - bypassing most armor! You also get a unique injury trait that can cause permanent damage or even death! However, they take a difficult amount of training to use well and are extremely rare, only granted by Rewards. The coolest feature of them is that you can summon and dismiss them once you've spent a week bonding with them, meaning you are never unarmed.

Radiant Shardblade? RAFO.

### Fabrials and Magical Items

Fabrials are a lot like magic items in other systems, but are reliant on Stormlight to work. For instance, a 50 mark heatrial can heat a small space but takes up Stormlight energy to do so. More and more powerful Fabrials, like Soulcasters expend Stormlight much faster but can do utterly fantastic things. 

A special Fabrial is available, a Freechair, that is an assistive device to help those with mobility issues to get around. It's encourage to make this free and low energy cost by GMs to those characters who otherwise have limited mobility.
